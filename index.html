Pillars of "Bangladesh 2.0"

Innovation

The Bangladesh July Student Movement of 2018 was not just a protest—it was a pivotal moment that highlighted the potential of youth-driven change to address systemic issues and inspire progress across multiple dimensions.

Sustainability

At its core, the movement called for sustainable solutions to Bangladesh's road safety crisis. Students demanded systemic changes, including stricter enforcement of traffic laws, transparency in licensing, and the modernization of transportation systems.

Infrastructure

The protests revealed the urgent need for infrastructure reforms, particularly in transportation. The students highlighted the risks posed by poorly maintained roads, unregulated public transport, and corruption in the licensing system. Their calls for improvement resonated as a broader demand for modern, efficient, and safe infrastructure.

Empowerment

Perhaps the most profound legacy of the July Student Movement was the empowerment of Bangladesh’s youth. The students demonstrated remarkable courage, organization, and leadership, proving that young people are not just the leaders of tomorrow but active changemakers of today.

The Vision of Future

Bangladesh 2.0 aims to redefine the nation’s trajectory by embracing cutting-edge technology, promoting inclusive growth, and ensuring sustainability. With initiatives in renewable energy, digital transformation, and robust infrastructure, the country is set to become a global model of development.

Digital Transformation

Creating a nationwide digital ecosystem to enhance connectivity and access to services for all citizens.

Green Energy

Investments in solar and wind energy to ensure a sustainable and eco-friendly future for the world.

Modern Infrastructure

Building state-of-the-art infrastructure to support economic growth and global competitiveness.
